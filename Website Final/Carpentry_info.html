What does being a carpenter mean?

Being a carpenter means you mainly work with wood or other, similar materials in order to repair or install foundations,walls, roofs or windows in buildings. You will also be expected to work with a wide variety of tools, ranging between a manual handsaw all the way to heavier machinery that requires some more experience.

Wages/Salary:

In New Zealand, new carpenters could expect to earn anything between $20, to $23 per hour (it depends on your employer/the type of work you do)
If you are a more experienced carpenter, you could expect to earn anything between $25, to $50 per hour

Job opportunities:

When it comes to the demand for carpenters in New Zealand, you would be happy to find that, according to CareersNz, the chances of you getting a job as a carpenter are good, due to there being a shortage of trade occupations, such as electricians, carpenters, builders, etc.

Training length:

The amount of time required for you to gain the necessary qualifications would likely vary depending on where you are performing your training, but the average length of time it takes to gain a qualification is between 3 to 4 years.
